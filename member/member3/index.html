Biography

I was born in August 1992 and currently hold the position of Associate Researcher at the Institute of Atmospheric Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences. My research focuses on satellite remote sensing of atmospheric aerosols and their climatic and environmental impacts. Here are some highlights of my work:

  1. Pioneered an early method for quantitatively retrieving nighttime aerosols based on city light.
  2. Developed a statistical learning-based aerosol interpolation method.
  3. Established an aerosol retrieval algorithm based on transfer learning.

I have published 24 papers, with 10 as the first/corresponding author, published in journals such as Engineering, Atmospheric Research and Atmospheric Environment. 281 citations in SCI journals. Currently, I am leading a project funded by the National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC), titled “Satellite Moonlight Remote Sensing of Nighttime Aerosol Optical Thickness” (Grant No. 42105128), spanning from January 2022 to December 2024, with a budget of 300,000 RMB.
